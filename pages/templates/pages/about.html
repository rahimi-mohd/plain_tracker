🚀 About Plain Tracker

💡 Why I built this

Back when I was interning as a Quality Assurance tester, I jumped between a bunch of small projects. The biggest headache? bug tracking.

Fancy trackers had a paywall (and way too many features no one touched), while some teams just hacked together a Google Sheet (which worked… kinda, but had zero communication tools like comments).

So, I thought: why not make something simple, clean, and actually fun to use? That’s how Plain Tracker was born — not to replace the giants, but to give people a straight-to-the-point alternative.

👥 Who it’s for

Anyone! From solo users keeping track of daily stuff, to small or medium teams that don’t want to wrestle with enterprise software. It’s not just for developers — if you need to track progress, ideas, or even your plant-watering schedule 🌱, Plain Tracker’s got your back.

⚙️ How to use it

Right now, you can play with it here on the site — but heads up: trackers are public for now (private mode coming soon 👀).

If you’re a DIY kind of person, you can also grab the source code from GitHub, and run it locally or host it anywhere you like.
